This M.Sc. research project investigates how component design and operating conditions influence the performance of a multistage humidification–dehumidification (HDH) desalination system.

## Scope

- Experimental evaluation of condenser configurations and surface-enhancement strategies
- Analysis of air-flow and heat-transfer behaviour
- Development and validation of a thermal process model
- Integration of the desalination process with solar-energy supply
- System-level parametric analysis

## Methods and tools

Experimental measurements, heat- and mass-transfer analysis, gPROMS, MATLAB, and process-model validation.

The project was conducted during an exchange research period at the University of Bradford as part of the M.Sc. programme at Politecnico di Milano.
